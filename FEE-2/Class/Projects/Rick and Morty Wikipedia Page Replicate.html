Premise

Rick and Morty follows the misadventures of cynical mad scientist Rick Sanchez and his good-hearted but fretful grandson Morty Smith, who split their time between domestic life and interdimensional adventures.

Characters

  • Rick Sanchez: An eccentric and alcoholic mad scientist who is the father of Beth Smith and the grandfather of Morty and Summer. He is often reckless, nihilistic, and selfish, but also displays moments of compassion and caring for his family.
  • Morty Smith: Rick's good-hearted but easily influenced grandson who is frequently dragged into Rick's misadventures. Morty is often reluctant to participate in Rick's schemes and adventures.
  • Beth Smith: Rick's daughter and the mother of Morty and Summer. Beth is a horse surgeon and is often depicted as being highly intelligent and ambitious, but also insecure and emotionally distant.
  • Jerry Smith: Beth's husband and Morty and Summer's father. Jerry is often portrayed as incompetent and insecure, but he genuinely cares for his family.
  • Summer Smith: Morty's older sister who occasionally joins Rick and Morty on their adventures. Summer is more self-assured and ambitious than Morty, but she also craves Rick's approval.

Episodes

As of the current date, "Rick and Morty" has aired six seasons with a total of 61 episodes. The episodes are known for their unique blend of science fiction, dark humor, and philosophical undertones.

Production

The series was created by Justin Roiland and Dan Harmon. Roiland voices the main characters, Rick and Morty. The show's concept originated from a short parody film of "Back to the Future" created by Roiland for Channel 101, a short film festival co-founded by Harmon.

Reception

"Rick and Morty" has received critical acclaim for its originality, creativity, and humor. It has developed a dedicated fanbase and has been praised for its character development and the performances of its voice cast.
